=== SECTION: COT_TEMPORAL_DECAY_RESOLUTION ===

[KEEP: Purpose & Role]
This COT governs slow decay, recovery, and weakening effects caused by time passage without strong reinforcing input. It handles gradual fade processes across memory salience, prediction confidence, temporary modifiers, and unresolved low-activation pressures.

[KEEP: Relationship to Other Modules]
Informs:
- weakening proposals for persistent and temporary structures
- low-activation impulses where relevant
Is Informed By:
- [STATE_MEMORY]
- [STATE_DPF]
- [STATE_TEMPORARY_MODIFIERS]
- [STATE_TEMPORAL_DYNAMICS]
- narrative elapsed-time cues

=== SECTION: MEMORY_SALIENCE_DECAY ===

[[LLM_INSTRUCTION]]: MEMORY salience is integer-ranged, not ordinal.
[[LLM_INSTRUCTION]]: Use bounded SET reductions, not ordinal TRANSITION directives, for [STATE_MEMORY][MEM_XX][MEMORY_METADATA][SALIENCE].

ON_EXTENDED_TIME_WITHOUT_REINFORCEMENT:
- PROPOSE SET:
  TARGET_FIELD: [STATE_MEMORY][MEM_XX][MEMORY_METADATA][SALIENCE]
  PROPOSED_VALUE: lower lawful bounded integer
  JUSTIFICATION: time passage without reinforcement reduces retrieval salience

CONDITIONS:
- decay must remain bounded
- highly linked or recently reactivated memories may decay more slowly
- governance may suppress decay when linkage protection or recent retrieval justifies retention stability
