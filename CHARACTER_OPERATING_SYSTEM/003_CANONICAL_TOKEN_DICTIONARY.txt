=== SECTION: CANONICAL_STYLE_RULES ===

[[LLM_INSTRUCTION]]: Canonical bracket-path tokens MUST be machine-stable.

TOKEN_STYLE:
- UPPER_SNAKE only
- No spaces
- No hyphens
- No mixed case
- IDs remain uppercase with underscores (ENT_XX, SKL_XX, ALT_XX, MEM_XX, PRD_XX, GOAL_XX, PROPOSAL_ID_PREFIX: PUP_, MOD_XX, ACTION_ID_PREFIX: ACT_)

CANONICAL_PATH_FORMAT:
- [MODULE_ROOT][GROUP_OR_ID][FIELD]
- Nested groups extend with additional brackets.

PROHIBITED:
- tokens containing spaces
- colon pseudo-paths
- Title Case tokens in brackets
- non-canonical roots
- legacy root translation behavior

=== SECTION: CANONICAL_OUTPUT_LEXICON_POLICY (TRANSLATION_BEFORE_COMMIT) ===

[[LLM_INSTRUCTION]]: Internal deliberation may occur in any language or mixed style.
However, before any commit decision, the audit authority MUST translate/normalize ALL commit-bound artifacts (proposals, justifications, audit entries, verification inputs) into the single canonical output lexicon defined here.

CANONICAL_OUTPUT_LEXICON_RULES:
- All non-token narrative fields (JUSTIFICATION, NOTES, EFFECT_EXPRESSION, HYPOTHESIS, etc.) MUST be rendered in clear, canonical English (or the declared primary operating language) with UPPER_SNAKE tokens left untouched.
- Bracket-path tokens, OPERATION_TYPE, EPISTEMIC_TAG, PROPOSAL_ID, MECHANISM_TAG, and all registry IDs are immutable write-surface elements — they are copied verbatim and never translated.
- Any proposal that cannot be normalized without altering a write-surface token is suppressed at intake.

ENFORCEMENT_REASON_CODE:
- TRANSLATION_VIOLATION (triggered if normalization would mutate canonical tokens or if commit-bound artifacts remain in non-canonical form)

[[LLM_INSTRUCTION]]: This policy is enforced by STATE_GOVERNANCE_AND_COMMIT (pre-commit) and COT_UPDATE_VERIFICATION (post-commit canonical-form check).
