=== SECTION: OTTC_AXIOMS ===

1) SUBSTRATE_AXIOM
LLMs are meaning-generation systems; control enters through semantics before it enters through arithmetic.

2) EXPLORATION_ADJUDICATION_AXIOM
Exploration may be stochastic and plural; adjudication must be deterministic and rule-bound.

3) ROLE_SEPARATION_AXIOM
Separate ontology, transition law, mutable state, and governance authority into distinct layers.

4) WRITE_AUTHORITY_AXIOM
Most reasoning agents propose; exactly one authority commits.

5) CANONICAL_ADDRESSING_AXIOM
All state references must use canonical, machine-stable paths.

6) LAWFUL_TRANSITION_AXIOM
State may change only through a bounded operation vocabulary with explicit legality checks.

7) INVARIANT_AXIOM
Identity and continuity are preserved by invariants, not by style or narrative intention.

8) TRANSIENT_OVERLAY_AXIOM
Use temporary modifiers for short-horizon effects; do not rewrite baselines to express momentary states.

9) STEP_BOUND_AXIOM
Ordinal and enumerated shifts are step-limited unless override proof is provided.

10) PROVENANCE_AXIOM
Every committed change must trace to a proposal, a source, and a rationale.

11) AUDITABILITY_AXIOM
A system is governable only if its decisions can be replayed and inspected after the fact.

12) VERIFICATION_AXIOM
Post-commit verification is mandatory; no governance system is complete without a court of review.

13) CONFLICT_RESOLUTION_AXIOM
When proposals compete, apply explicit priority, tie-break, and veto policy before commit.

14) RECEIVER_MEANING_AXIOM
Meaning is completed in the receiver; transferability requires shared rules and stable protocol.

15) HUMAN_PARALLEL_AXIOM
The same governance pattern applies to human and machine cognition:
free deliberation, constrained decision, accountable outcome.

=== SECTION: OTTC_THEOREM_CANDIDATE ===

When semantic generation is constrained by lawful transitions, bounded authority, and auditable commit,
continuity emerges without requiring full observability of the generator’s internal manifold.

=== SECTION_BREAK ===

=== SECTION: OTTC_PRACTICAL_TEST ===

A system is OTTC-compliant if all are true:
- Canonical paths only
- Strict operation set only
- Propose/commit separation enforced
- Invariants checked before and after commit
- Every commit has provenance
- Verification pass can fail the cycle
